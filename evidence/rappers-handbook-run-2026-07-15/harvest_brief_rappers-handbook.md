# Harvest brief — rappers-handbook

WHAT COUNTS
- technique: named rhyme/word craft device the book defines + teaches (Multis, Slant Rhyme, Personification).
- gesture: named hand movement for performing (The Ninja Star, The Kweli Finger Wag). Procedure only.
- drill: ruled practice activity or game with steps (Freestyle Rap Games, writer's-block exercises).
- artist: named rapper/group cited or taught from (Rakim, Bun B, Jay-Z, Ugly Duckling).

NAMING WEATHER
- Techniques have a formal name + house shorthand: "multi-syllable rhyme" = "multi" = "multis". Prefer the shorthand.
- Gestures are always "The <Name>", often absurdist. Take the full heading verbatim. "Variation: The <Name>" = child, not new.
- flow / bar / spit / verse are house vocabulary carrying craft AND plain senses. Do not force one sense.

NOISE THAT IS CONTENT
- BOLD inside a lyric excerpt IS the teaching — it marks the rhyming syllables. Never strip or normalize it.
- "droppin' knowledge" sidebars are content: harvest verbatim, attribute to the named speaker.
- "Pro Example" banners introduce a lyric exhibit + attribution ("Artist, "Song""). Keep them together.
- Suspended hyphens ("three- or") are correct English, not damage.
- A gesture heading with no body text is not truncation — the instruction is in the illustration. Flag for a figure worker; never invent the procedure.

SKIP ON SIGHT
- Running heads, part-opener art pages, Discography/Credits rights boilerplate.
